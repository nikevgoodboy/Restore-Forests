Trees are vital for the planet, offering oxygen, shade, and wildlife habitat. Learn about tree care, planting, and their environmental importance in this FAQ.

Air Purification: Trees absorb pollutants like carbon dioxide (CO₂), sulfur dioxide, and nitrogen dioxide, releasing oxygen and improving air quality.

Process: Through photosynthesis, trees absorb CO₂, a major greenhouse gas, from the atmosphere and convert it into oxygen (O₂), reducing the overall concentration of CO₂ in the air.

Trees absorb carbon dioxide (CO₂) and release oxygen through photosynthesis. They also filter harmful pollutants like nitrogen dioxide (NO₂), sulfur dioxide (SO₂), and particulate matter, making the air cleaner and healthier to breathe.
